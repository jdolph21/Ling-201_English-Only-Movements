The English-Only Movement: A Misunderstanding of United States Language Policy

By: Isaac McConnell & Josh Dolph

Our Mission

In the United States today, there is a belief among some that English is the official language of the country and that it alone should be used in all communication. Due to this and other similar beliefs, at times there arise situations where those who hold to this ideology try to force or shame others into exclusively using English vs. their native language, including those who don't know or haven't achieved fluency in it. Our mission with this website is to debunk as many English Only myths as possible, explaining them from a linguistic perspective and introducing facts about linguistic and cultural diversity within the United States to undermine the credibility of these myths.

Language Myth-Reinforcing Examples

Language Myth-Reinforcing Examples

These images serve as examples of how language myths are perpetuated in society at large. It can be a flyer from a Dunkin' Donuts offering free food and drinks for correcting co-workers who aren't speaking English. It can be the former owner of Geno's subs in Philadelphia essentially saying that speaking a language other than English, since English is the official language, is worthy of deportation. It can take any form, but the message is usually based on a misunderstanding of United States language policy, which in fact doesn't prescribe an official language, and even if it did, it wouldn't result in an English requirement for every area of life. The above arguments illustrate the necessity for a deeper understanding of language use and requirements, or the lack thereof, within the United States. Until that occurs, examples such as these will continue to occur.

Myths

English is the official language of the United States

English Only policies improve proficiency in English

English is threatened by bilingual/multilingual speakers

Facts

The United States does not have an official language; 31 states do specify English as their official language, but not the country as a whole.

This available evidence suggests the opposite of this to be true; People educated in multiple languages actually are better at using English than monolingual speakers are

This is not supported by any evidence; English exists and thrives in many multilingual countries, with no sign of that changing soon

Myths

English is the official language of the United States

English Only policies improve proficiency in English

English is threatened by bilingual/multilingual speakers

English Only policies are effective at making English the only languge used

English Only policies would help unify the country

Spanish in the United States in purely a function of immigration in the 20th and 21st centuries

Facts

The United States does not have an official language; 31 states do specify English as their official language, but not the country as a whole.

The available evidence suggests the opposite of this to be true; People educated in multiple languages tend to be better at using English than monolingual speakers are

This is not supported by any evidence; English exists and thrives in many multilingual countries, with no sign of that changing soon

English Only policies would only have effect in government settings, where English is already the primary (and often only) language used; it would have no (legal) effect on language use in other scenarios

English Only policies would only 'unify' government settings, by excluding speakers of other languages; they would have a negative effect on unification, if any, outside of government settings

Spanish has been spoken in the United States for at least as long as English
